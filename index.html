Intro

Hi, I'm John Carrera. This site showcases some of the things I love doing most, like coding, and woodworking. Take a look around and feel free to let me know if there's anything you like. I am fluent in Java, Javascript, and Python as well as other languages and technologies like React, Redux, ORMs, databases, and more. I also have a fully outfitted production woodshop and can make custom furniture, art pieces, and special gifts that you can help design to match your style. Check out my full-stack projects or my woodworking portfolio.

Tech Projects

Below are a few of the full-stack web apps I've built from the ground up. In addition to the projects below, in my professional experience I have built applications for clients that include many companies in the fortune 500 and even the US Department of Justice. I can't post those here but there are plenty of details I can talk about. Feel free to contact me for more information.

cybertrade

Cybertrade is my favorite of these projects. It's a retro cyber-punk themed stock trading app that has working paper-trading features. Users can create an account that will start with fifty thousand dollars that they can use to buy and sell stocks of their choice, create and edit watchlists, and even view transaction history, assets and portfolio on thier personal dashboard! Check it out here.

Task Maker

Task Maker is a productivity to-do app that is based on the ever-popular 'Remember the Milk' Users here can create and manage tasks using lists and tags. The interface supports due dates, time estimation and many other features. It's a fully functional to-do app! Check it out here.

leet-up

leet-up is a fun clone of the popular site 'Meetup'. In my take on this classic platform, leet-up is based on the tech world and has both real-life and fictional hacker groups. Users can create an account, log in, and create their own groups and events! Check it out here

About

Hey! It's me again. Here's a little more about me and my passions for tech and wood!

Technology

In case you were wondering, yes I have always been into technology. My dad was in IT during the 'dot com' boom of the 90's and our family was always in the forefront of the tech world. Back in those times it was not common for a family to have a home computer, let alone 4 of them on a closed network! I was hosting LAN events and setting up my own networks from a very young age.

Not long after that, I managed to get my hands on a book about C++. I was only about ten or eleven years old so I begged my dad to get me an IDE. If I remember correctly there were not a lot of open-source free options at the time. He came through and managed to get a visual studio license from work. It was not long before I was writing my own terminal programs and well, The rest is history.

In my professional life I started in the IT world, working at managed IT companies, helpdesks for large organizations, and at one point as director for a small managed IT company. I was always looking for something a little more engaging, something that would allow me to flex my logic and creativity the way woodworking does. After moving to Miami, I met a friend of a friend that worked in legal technology consulting we exchanged information and one day he called me out of the blue. There was an opening and he wanted to know if I'd be interested in consulting.

I decided to give it a try and that is where I really found my calling. Shortly after starting, one of our bread-and-butter vendors released a REST API for their new cloud platform. Clients were eager to move to the cloud but somewhat nervous that they wouldn't have the same luxuries that they did with an on-site database. I spent some time on the side, reading the API docs and making a prototype to show that we could get all the same data and run the same queries using the API, it worked out great. I brought the prototype to my boss and from then on I was in charge of custom development for all client needs. That's when I knew my career was going right where I wanted it.

As the lead developer, I would either write the code myself or oversee it's development by other developers to ensure client satisfaction, deadlines and budget requirements. I left that job to start a psychology practice with my wife who is a Doctor of Clinical Psychology (Check us out!) knowing that I would eventually head to some form of continuing education before looking for my next role as a developer.

I attended the 6-month 1000+ hour course at App Academy where I improved my repertoire with some awesome full-stack web technologies like React and Redux as well as learning Javascript and Python. For a full list of my skills, click here. Now that the course is over. I'm looking for my next role in a company that shares my same values like innovation and creative thinking.
